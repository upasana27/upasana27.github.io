About

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at Arizona State University, advised by Prof. Subbarao Kambhampati. My work sits at the intersection of human–AI collaboration, multi-agent systems, and large language model (LLM)-based agents.

My research focuses on three main directions:

  • Analyzing cooperation in multi-agent systems, with a particular emphasis on understanding interactions between human and AI teammates and identifying when high performance does (and does not) imply true collaboration.
  • Designing human-aware adaptive agents by modeling human decision-making and leveraging hierarchical task representations to enable robust, generalizable coordination.
  • Studying human–LLM interaction, focusing on interpretability, trust, reasoning traces, and personalized communication in user-adaptive agentic systems.

Broadly, I aim to build principled foundations for human–AI multi-agent systems that are robust, adaptive, and grounded in structured models of reasoning.

Outside research, I am a trained Kathak dancer. I enjoy reading widely—especially history and philosophy—and I spend as much time as possible hiking.

Research

Nested Training for Mutual Adaptation in Human–AI Teaming

October 2025 – Present

Developed a nested training regime grounded in finitely nested I-POMDPs to enable structured mutual adaptation in human–AI teams. Demonstrated robust generalization to unseen adaptive partners. Extended Abstract accepted at 25th International Conference on Autonomous Agents and Multiagent Systems (AAMAS 2026).

Do Cognitively Interpretable Reasoning Traces Improve LLM Performance?

August 2025 – December 2025

Investigated the relationship between interpretability and task performance in LLM reasoning traces. Fine-tuned open-weight models and conducted human-subject evaluations. Presented at the NeurIPS 2025 CogInterp Workshop.

Who is Helping Whom? Analyzing Interdependencies to Evaluate Cooperation in Human–AI Teaming

September 2024 – July 2025

Proposed a metric of constructive interdependence to evaluate cooperation beyond task reward in human–AI teams. Accepted at AAAI 2026 and presented at RLC 2025.

On the Pitfalls of Learning to Cooperate with Self-Play Agents

August 2023 – December 2023

Examined the gap between self-play training and human collaboration in cooperative MARL. Late-Breaking Report at the ACM/IEEE International Conference on Human-Robot Interaction (HRI 2024).
