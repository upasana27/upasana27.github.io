📖 My Story

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at Arizona State University, advised by Prof. Subbarao Kambhampati at the Yochan Lab. My work sits at the intersection of human–AI collaboration, multi-agent systems, and large language model (LLM)-based agents.

My research focuses on three main directions:

🤝

Analyzing Cooperation in Multi-Agent Systems

Understanding interactions between human and AI teammates and identifying when high performance does (and does not) imply true collaboration.

🧠

Designing Human-Aware Adaptive Agents

Modeling human decision-making and leveraging hierarchical task representations to enable robust, generalizable coordination.

💬

Studying Human–LLM Interaction

Focusing on interpretability, trust, reasoning traces, and personalized communication in user-adaptive agentic systems.

Broadly, I aim to build principled foundations for human–AI multi-agent systems that are robust, adaptive, and grounded in structured models of reasoning.

Outside research, I am a trained Kathak dancer 💃. I enjoy reading widely—especially history and philosophy—and I spend as much time as possible hiking ⛰️.
